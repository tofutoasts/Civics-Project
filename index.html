What is sustainability?

Sustainability describes the practices that meet current needs without using up the resources available for future generations. A sustainable lifestyle minimizes one's carbon emissions, waste, and pollution. The concept of sustainable development was initially introduced in 1972. Sustainable efforts, however, became more widespread during the 1980s when the World Commission on Environment and Development popularized the term “sustainable development” after publishing the book Our Common Future tackling our global socioeconomic and ecological issues. However, recent innovation and technology have allowed people to transition into a more sustainable lifestyle. Reusable water bottles, electric vehicles, and even solar panels have been integrated into peoples everyday lives, all of which contribute to conservation of resources and elimination of pollution.

Lack of Accessibility

Despite efforts to be sustainable becoming more widespread, global efforts have been disproportionately effective according to financial resources because “going green” tends to be expensive and time consuming. Sustainable efforts often connect with consumerism: In order to live a heavily sustainable lifestyle, one must actively purchase numerous products that cost more than their unsustainable alternatives. Many people do not have the financial means to maintain, let alone achieve, a sustainable lifestyle. Buying electric cars, solar panels, compost bins, and more can be quite costly. Even taking the time to look into buying these products can be unrealistic to those with tight schedules.In short, sustainable options are most accessible to the financially secure. Although living a thoroughly sustainable lifestyle isnt attainable for most, there are still small tasks that nearly any person can complete in their everyday life.

How to take action:

An easy way to start is by recycling cans and various plastic items. A simple recycling bin will have a great impact on the environment, especially considering the amount of plastic found in the ocean. If everyone takes control of where their trash goes, then a collective effort can make a big difference for the world. Another simple way is by reducing your energy usage. Turning off the lights after you leave a room will decrease energy production, the pollution used to make it, and will help you save a lot of money in the long run. A method of energy saving is installing solar panels on the roof of your house, but installations are pricey and not everyone can afford it. Monitoring your energy usage is simple and free! Water usage is a second factor that can drain your money and strain the environment. Taking quick showers or turning off the sink while you brush your teeth is an easy way to start. In most areas, we see water as a continuous source when in reality, our resources are steadily depleting. Being mindful of how much water you use versus how much water you actually need is a lifesaver for both you and the world.
